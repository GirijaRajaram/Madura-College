Department Of Zoology

ESTD - 1966

Mission & Vision



  • To design and update the curriculum, bearing in mind the relevance to regional and national requirements and expectations.

  • To impart quality education in the field of Zoology, enabling our students to confidently face the job market.

  • To inculcate the spirit of scientific temper in the minds of the students.

  • To develop in the minds of students a concern for the need to conserve natural resources and to inspire them to spread this message to the society.

  • To sensitize the students the need for keeping the environment clean.



Courses offered

The Department of Zoology was carved out of the then existing “Natural Science Department” in the year 1966 when B.Sc., Zoology Major Course was started. From the year 1991 M.Sc., Zoology is offered under the self-financing stream. The crowning glory came in 1994 when the Department was recognized as a Centre for Research in Zoology by the MADURAI KAMARAJ UNIVERSITY

Course highlights

The course is designed in such a way that students are taught the basics as well as specialized papers such as Bio-diversity& Conservation Biology, Bio-physics, Bio-statistics & Bio-informatics

Laboratory

The department has a well-equipped laboratory.

Library

The departmental library is equipped with latest books on regular topics as well as special topics.

Research & Extension

The department has successfully carried out 8 major research projects funded by agencies (UGC / CSIR / DoEn / DST / CES / IISc / DBT) amounting to Rs.30 lakhs. Evolved a user friendly key for the identification of aquatic macro invertebrates for the benefit of students and field workers.

The Research Centre was awarded the status of a nodal centre by the department of Ecological Sciences of the premier Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore under WGBN project of Pew foundation, USA for the inventorying of the biodiversity of the Western ghats and the Eastern ghats from 1994.

Further it is a Referral centre for identification of aquatic insects.

As on April 2009, 6 research scholars have been awarded Ph.D .

The Department, having already been recognized as a leading centre for studies on Aquatic Entomology aims to further its expertise by continuing exhaustive investigations on aspects of inventorying and monitoring the diversity of aquatic insects of southern Tamilnadu with a view to prioritize the conservation of lotic and lentic habitats of Montane and non - montane areas and to contribute to the consolidation of National Biodiversity Register (NBR). Further, certificate courses in Clinical Lab Technology and Bioinformatics (in collaboration with Computer Science Department) are proposed
