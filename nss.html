NSS


The College has 4 girls units and 3 boys units in the College

Motto of N.S.S.: “NOT ME BUT YOU”





This expresses the essence of democratic living and upholds the need for selfless service and appreciation of the other man's point of view and also to show consideration for fellow human beings.

The Symbols


The symbols of NSS are based on the "Rath" wheel of the Konark Sun temple of Orissa. It stands for continuity as well as change and implies on the part of the NSS for continuous striving forward for social transformation and upliftment.


AIMS OF N.S.S.:



      To develop a national consciousness among the youth                                  

      To generate social awareness among the students                                          

      To promote the dignity of labour among the educated                                    

      To strengthen the spirit of service and sacrifice in the younger generation

The Volunteers





The National Service Scheme (NSS) is a program organized by the Ministry of Education and Youth Service to afford opportunities and training in service to the community and to the nation, for students with an aptitude. Every NSS volunteer shall know his/her duties and responsibilities. He/she shall be courteous, and patient. Each student attending the NSS will be required to put in 120 hours of service activity each academic year and he will be expected to maintain a diary showing details of his participation duly countersigned by the Programme Organizers and willing to serve the community in all possible ways.

In order to achieve the objectives the volunteers are involved in the following thrust areas for implementing their skills by the way of active involvement. The activities of the NSS volunteers include: rendering assistance in the local hospitals, regulating the traffic, lending tutorial and scribe work for blind students ,cleaning up the Campus, organizing blood donation camps etc.,
In the CBCS system, 2 Credits are awarded to students enrolling & doing service under NSS



Credit Eligibility:

  • 120 hours of service in two academic years / Special camping programme for seven  days
  • Participation in National level or State level camps

Regular Projects:

  • Traffic regulation
  • Blind assistance
  • Medical project
  • Garments collection
  • Special coaching classes to the school children
  • Helping social welfare institution
  • Rural upliftment
  • Non-formal education
  • Blood donation
  • Legal awareness
  • Health awareness
